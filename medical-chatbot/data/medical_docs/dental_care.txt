Dental Care and Common Dental Conditions

Tooth Pain and Cavities:
- Cavities are permanently damaged areas in the hard surface of teeth
- Cavity symptoms include sharp pain, sensitivity to hot/cold, visible holes
- Tooth pain can range from mild discomfort to severe pain
- Treatment depends on extent: fluoride treatments, fillings, or root canals

Dental Trauma:
- Includes tooth knocked out, fractured tooth, broken tooth, chipped tooth
- Immediate care within 30 minutes is crucial for knocked-out teeth
- Keep tooth moist, contact dentist immediately

Gum Disease:
- Symptoms: bleeding gums, gum pain, gum infection, gum disease, receding gums
- Caused by plaque buildup and bacterial infection
- Early stage: gingivitis (treatable); advanced: periodontitis

Dental Abscess:
- Bacterial infection causing pus collection
- Symptoms: severe tooth/gum pain, swelling jaw, fever
- Requires urgent dental intervention to prevent spread

Bruxism (Teeth Grinding):
- Can lead to worn teeth, jaw pain, headaches
- Often stress-related

Preventive Dental Care:
- Regular brushing, flossing reduces cavity risk
- Professional cleanings every 6 months recommended
- Proper diet limits sugary foods
